Important! Please read

Readability formulas are used in determining the complexity of a given text. It usually returns a grade level as an output that corresponds to the number of years in education required to understand the text, hence the reason why it is called 'readability'. It measures how easy or difficult it is to read a particular text.

This webite application is created by the researchers in an attempt to assess these various readability formulas. All of the formulas listed below yield results that measure the grade level required to understand the text. For instance, a result of 12 would mean people who attained a Grade 12 education can easily understand or read the text. Any higher than that would require education at the College level or can be translated into the number of years of education required to read the text without difficulty. So a result of 14 would then mean that 14 years of education is required, and so on and so forth. Keep in mind that each formula may not yield the same result as the other.

As a respondent, you can test these formulas out and see for yourself. It is recommended that you try each formula one by one in order for you to appropriately answer the survey that was given to you. If you find any bugs or errors, please contact the researchers through email, text, or messenger. You can find their contact details on the contacts page. Have fun!
